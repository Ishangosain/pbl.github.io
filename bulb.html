BULB

Use bulbs as plant vase

1)Things You Need:-For the vase: old light bulbs needle nose pliers nail set hammer

2)Breaking the back part of bulb:- use the nail set and hammer to break the black part of the bulb be careful, it is glass.

3)Removing the Silver Layer of Bulb:- Removing the Silver Layer of Bulb Remove the small silver layer with your pliers. Tap out black glass into garbage. Break the next layer of glass inside the bulb carefully, using the nail set and hammer again. Break off any little pieces around the inside edge of the bulb.

4)Removing the guts of the Bulb:- Remove the guts of the bulb with your needle nose pliers. Shake out any loose glass. You now have a vase.

5)Stand/Hanger for the Bulb:- Drill a 1 by 8 inch hole in the dowel or big enough for your wire to fit it in.Mike also wrapped a few layers of electrical tape around the dowel because the dowel was not as big as the light bulb base. (You will also be able to adjust the size after removing the wire from the dowel). Cut your wire into 20cm pieces. Mike used the wire that you use to hang drop ceilings with. Put wire into the hole in the dowel and start wrapping the wire around the dowel clockwise. Wrap it 2-3 times. Use the wire cutters to remove the wire from the dowel, cutting right at the hole. Check to see if your bulb will fit into coils, adjust if needed by turning the wire with pliers to either tighten or loosen.

6)Hanging the Vase:- Thread bulb into wire, it should thread in and tighten up - like putting a bulb in a light socket. Bend wire down to form a base, shaping it how you like. To make a hanging vase, follow the steps above for preparing the light bulb. Then drill two holes in the bulb base and attach floral wire. Enjoy your new cute, upcycled vase! Have a happy day!
